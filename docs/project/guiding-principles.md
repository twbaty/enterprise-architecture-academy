# Guiding principles

## Teach the problem before the framework

Begin with a recognizable enterprise problem. Introduce framework concepts only after the learner understands the need they address.

## Architecture is decision support

Every concept must connect to a decision, stakeholder concern, trade-off, governance need, or change outcome.

## Certification is a milestone, not the destination

The curriculum must prepare learners for certification while developing judgment that remains useful after the examination.

## Foundation and Practitioner remain distinct

Foundation emphasizes identifying, defining, explaining, distinguishing, and understanding. Practitioner emphasizes applying, analyzing, tailoring, evaluating, defending, and governing.

## Use persistent scenarios

Reuse the same fictional enterprises across lessons so learners can see architecture accumulate and change over time.

## Technical credibility without product captivity

Examples may use real technology patterns, but architectural reasoning must not collapse into vendor selection or product operation.

## Minimum useful architecture

More artifacts are not inherently better. Produce enough structure and evidence to make, communicate, and govern the decision.

## Reality outranks the model

Baseline architecture must be evidence-based. Assumptions, unknowns, intended state, and actual state must remain distinguishable.

## Operational viability is architectural

Reliability, supportability, observability, continuity, security, cost, and delivery constraints are architecture concerns.

## Governance must enable delivery

Governance means decision rights, accountability, exception handling, compliance, and feedback—not ceremony for its own sake.

## Canonical source prevents drift

Markdown in Git is authoritative. Other formats are generated outputs, not parallel source documents.

## Open contribution does not eliminate editorial ownership

Anyone may contribute. The repository owner determines what enters the curriculum and when it is published.

## Preserve useful work

Existing documents, discussions, and decisions must be migrated, inventoried, or deliberately retired. Useful content should not disappear merely because the publishing format changes.

## Build substance before presentation

Information architecture, traceability, curriculum completeness, and instructional quality take precedence over visual polish.

# Glossary

Precise, exam-grade definitions for terms used across the curriculum. Lessons introduce these concepts in plain language and link here for the exact definition; use this page when you need the precise wording rather than the intuition.

Terminology is restated in original language, consistent with the current TOGAF Standard published by The Open Group. Wording has been refined across TOGAF editions — check the current official glossary at [opengroup.org/togaf](https://www.opengroup.org/togaf) before relying on exact current wording for exam preparation.

**Architecture.** Used two ways in TOGAF: (1) a formal description of a system and a plan of its components, detailed enough to guide implementation; or (2) the structure of components, their relationships, and the principles governing their design and evolution over time.

**Architecture Building Block (ABB).** A component of the architecture stated as a requirement or capability — what is needed — independent of any specific product or vendor.

**Architecture Development Method (ADM).** TOGAF's core method: a cyclical, iterative sequence of phases for developing architecture from vision through implementation and ongoing change. See F3.

**Architecture domain.** One of four conventional divisions of architecture work, often abbreviated BDAT: Business, Data, Application, Technology.

**Architecture Landscape.** A description of what is actually deployed and in flight right now, maintained as part of the Architecture Repository — distinct from a target architecture, which describes a future state.

**Architecture Partitioning.** The discipline of splitting enterprise architecture work into pieces — by business unit, geography, or domain — that can run semi-independently without losing enterprise-level coherence.

**Architecture principle.** A general rule or guidance statement, defined once and referenced repeatedly, governing how resources are used across the enterprise.

**Baseline architecture.** A description of the enterprise as it currently stands.

**Business Transformation Readiness Assessment.** A technique for evaluating whether an organization can actually absorb a planned architectural change — sponsorship, skills, capacity to run old and new states in parallel — independent of whether the architecture itself is technically sound.

**Capability.** An ability that an organization, person, or system possesses, usually stated at a general, high level, requiring some combination of organization, people, process, and technology to achieve.

**Concern.** What a specific stakeholder cares about — cost, risk, delivery time, compliance exposure, and so on.

**Enterprise.** Whatever collection of organizations shares a common set of goals. Not necessarily a whole company; can be a division, or a cross-functional group formed around one initiative.

**Enterprise Architecture.** The practice of applying architecture, in either TOGAF sense of the word, at enterprise scope, to help the enterprise reach its objectives.

**Gap.** A stated difference between the baseline architecture and the target architecture — something the target requires that the baseline lacks or has differently.

**Solution Building Block (SBB).** A real, specific implementation — a named product or configuration — that fulfills one or more Architecture Building Blocks.

**Stakeholder.** Anyone with an interest in the architecture.

**Target architecture.** A description of the enterprise as it is intended to be at a future point.

**View.** A representation of the architecture, built from a viewpoint, produced for a specific stakeholder to address their concern.

**Viewpoint.** A definition of how to construct a view that addresses a given kind of concern.

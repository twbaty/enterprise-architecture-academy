# Foundation Curriculum Design

This is the living architecture of the Foundation course.

## Source for this structure

This module list is based on the actual TOGAF Standard, 10th Edition Foundation certification structure — specifically, the module breakdown published by Good e-Learning, an Open Group Accredited Training Course provider, cross-checked against independently published exam domain weightings. It replaces an earlier draft that organized Foundation as one lesson per ADM phase (Phase A, then B/C/D, then E, F, G, H individually). That phase-by-phase depth is not Foundation content under the current edition — it belongs to the Practitioner certification. The real Foundation exam treats all ADM phases together, at overview depth, in a single module, and instead devotes separate modules to ADM techniques, governance, applying the method, and architecture content.

This also means Foundation is genuinely shorter than the earlier 14-lesson draft assumed — nine lessons, not fourteen.

## Current lesson sequence

1. Enterprise Architecture from First Principles — why the discipline exists, before any TOGAF vocabulary.
2. The TOGAF Standard: Structure, Concepts, and Vocabulary — core terminology and the standard's major parts.
3. The Architecture Development Method: The Whole Cycle — the ADM's ring shape, Requirements Management as the continuous hub, and the purpose of every phase (Preliminary through H) at overview depth.
4. ADM Techniques, Principles, and Stakeholder Management — architecture principles, stakeholder analysis, business scenarios, interoperability, Business Transformation Readiness Assessment, and risk, at a Foundation level.
5. Applying the ADM: Iteration, Landscape, and Architecture Capability — iteration in practice, Architecture Landscape, Architecture Partitioning, and the purpose of an Enterprise Architecture capability, including a note on agile/digital enterprise practice.
6. Architecture Governance — the Architecture Board, Architecture Contracts, and Compliance, at overview level, distinct from the deeper implementation-governance judgment calls covered in Practitioner.
7. Architecture Content: Deliverables, Artifacts, and Building Blocks — catalogs, matrices, diagrams, and the ABB/SBB distinction in depth.
8. Enterprise Continuum, Architecture Repository, and Reference Models — classification from generic to organization-specific, and TOGAF's reference models.
9. Foundation Integration and Exam Readiness — cumulative review and practice.

**Exit criterion:** Every Foundation certification learning outcome has an assigned lesson, and a learner completing all nine can sit the TOGAF Enterprise Architecture Foundation exam.
